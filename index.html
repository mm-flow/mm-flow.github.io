Motivation

Gaussian → Gaussian (Independent Coupling)
The classifc flow matching paradigm where the target distribution (e.g., SD-VAE 1 latent space) is unimodal Gaussian.
GMM → GMM (Independent Coupling)
Estimate a data-adaptive mixture source distribution from the multimodal target distribution as a warm start.
Gaussian → GMM (Independent Coupling)
Paradigm shift to structured latent space (e.g., RAE 2, MAETok 3, SVGTok 4) that reduces the learning complexity.
GMM → GMM (Mode Coupling)
Co-design with mode-dependent coupling achieves shortest and straightest sampling trajectories.
Remark: These sampling trajectories in the animation are generated from pre-trained flow matching models on 2D simulation data. The training and sampling code are modified from Diffusion-Explorer 5. The straightness of the sampling trajectories correlates with the level of crossing during training 67. Our method reduces crossing and thus leads to straighter trajectories.

Our Method (MM-FM)

MM-FM fits a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) on the target distribution \(p(Z_1)\) as the source distribution \(p(Z_0) = \sum_{i=1}^m c_i\mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \Sigma_i)\) and apply mode-dependent data coupling \(\pi_{\textrm{mode}}(Z_0, Z_1)=p(Z_1)p(Z_0 \mid Z_1)\) for training. To mitigate the concentration of measure and curse of dimensionality in high dimension, we propose a subroutine for image data to fit a GMM on [CLS] token in \(\mathbb{R}^{768}\) to obtain a operational GMM on patch tokens in \(\mathbb{R}^{768\times16\times16}\).

Experimental Results

On ImageNet256 dataset, without any conditioning (i.e., learning a purely unconditional velocity field), MM-FM (FID=3.82) without guidance outperforms the baseline using Gaussian source distribution and independent coupling (FID=9.33) on 80 epochs. It also outperforms the unconditional generation result (FID=4.96) with guidance on 200 epochs reported in RAE2.

Comparing with unconditional generation systems (i.e., without using annotated class labels) without guidance, MM-FM with mode conditioning (FID=3.18) on 80 epochs outperforms DLC16 with DLC conditioning (FID=5.75) on 80 epochs and RCG15 with MoCov3 conditioning (FID=4.89) on 400 epochs.

Though not relying on annotated class labels, MM-FM delivers matching and superior performance comparing with class-conditioning systems (including E2E-VAE11, VA-VAE12, MAETok3, AlignTok13, VFM-VAE14, and SVGTok4) in early checkpoints.

With AutoGuidance17, MM-FM trained for 80 epochs only without using annotated class labels achieves state-of-the-art FID=2.74 generation performance on ImageNet256.

Besides training efficiency, MM-FM achieves inference efficiency due to reduced crossing of flows during training time. As a result, it has straighter sampling trajectories and thus requires fewer Euler steps of an ODE sampler. The figure 4 shows that MM-FM requires approxiamtely 5x fewer steps to achieve comparable generation quality relative to Gaussian source distribution with independent coupling.

The improvement is monotonic with respect to increasing number of modes toward the degree of reasonable GMM estimation with sufficient data points per mode. Interestingly, the optimal # modes isn't 1000, suggesting that the underlying data manifold contains richer intrinsic structure beyond the categorization provided by annotated labels, which MM-FM can effectively capture. Main Theoretical Results

We analyze multimodal generation with matched mixture distributions:

Target Distribution
\[q(z) = \sum_{k=1}^{m} c_k \tilde{q}(\Sigma_k^{-1/2}(z-\mu_k))\]
Source Distribution (Ours)
\[p(z) = \sum_{k=1}^{m} c_k \tilde{p}(\Sigma_k^{-1/2}(z-\mu_k))\]

Here \(\tilde{q}\) and \(\tilde{p}\) are unimodal base distributions (e.g., standard Gaussians), and the mixtures are formed by placing transformed copies at locations \(\mu_k\) with covariances \(\Sigma_k\). Both \(p\) and \(q\) share the same parameters \(\{\mu_k,\Sigma_k\}_{k=1}^{m}\).

We compare two coupling strategies: mode coupling \(\pi_{\text{mode}}\) (our approach, where each source mode \(p_k\) flows only to the corresponding target mode \(q_k\)) vs. independent coupling \(\pi_{\text{ind}}\) (standard approach, where source and target are sampled independently). We measure generation complexity by:

Straightness
\[\mathbb{E}[\int_0^1 \|u_t(Z_t)\|^2 dt - \|\int_0^1 u_t(Z_t) dt\|^2]\]
Total Length
\[\mathbb{E}[\int_0^1 \|u_t(Z_t)\| dt]\]
Lipschitz Constant
\[\sup_z \|\nabla u_t(z)\|_{\text{op}}\]

Assumption 1

  1. The support \(\Omega\) of \(\tilde{p}\) and \(\tilde{q}\) is bounded and convex, and its affine images \(\{\Omega_k\}_{k \in [m]}\) are mutually disjoint. For every pair \(j,k \in [m]\) with \(j \neq k\) and every \(z \in \Omega_k\):
    \[ \|z-\mu_k\|_{\Sigma_k^{-1/2}} < \text{dist}_{\Sigma_j^{-1/2}}(z,\Omega_j) \]
  2. The velocity field \(\tilde{u}_t\) bridging \(\tilde{p}\) and \(\tilde{q}\) is Lipschitz continuous for every \(t \in (0,1)\), and its Jacobian is defined globally in \(\Omega\).

Theorem 1

Under Assumption 1, with multimodal prior \(p\) and mode coupling \(\pi_{\text{mode}}\):

  1. \[ \text{Straightness}(p,q;\pi_{\text{mode}}) \leq C \cdot \text{Straightness}(\tilde{p},\tilde{q};\pi_{\text{ind}}), \] where \(C := \sum_{k=1}^{m} c_k \|\Sigma_k\|_{\text{op}} \leq 1\).
  2. \[ \text{Length}(p,q;\pi_{\text{mode}}) \leq \left(\sum_{k=1}^{m} c_k \|\Sigma_k\|^{1/2}_{\text{op}}\right) \cdot \text{Length}(\tilde{p},\tilde{q};\pi_{\text{ind}}) \]
  3. \[ \text{Lip}(u_t) \leq \text{Lip}(\tilde{u}_t), \quad \forall t \in (0,1) \]

Interpretation: When using the multimodal prior and the mode coupling, the straightness, total length, and velocity field Lipschitz constant of the multimodal generation problem never exceed that of the unimodal generation problem.

For additional theoretical results and complete proofs, please refer to our paper.

Conclusion

We propose MM-FM, a co-design of a multimodal source distribution and mode coupling that exploits structured latent space imposed by foundation models. It delivers faster convergence and superior generation performance compared to the classifc flow matching recipe that stays on unimodal Gaussian and independent coupling. We wish it provides a principled flow design for the co-evolution of foundation models and generative models. As more foundation models emerges across domains, training flow-based generative models can be made efficient by MM-FM.

For curious readers, we recommend Flow Matching Tutorial Guide and Rectified Flow Tutorial for flow matching basics. For the insights into structured latents and training efficiency of flow-based models, we also recommend SVG and RAE's project page.
